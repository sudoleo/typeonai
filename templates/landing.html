Consensus Answer

The Nile is the conventional longest river, but the Amazon has a credible claim.

The models converge on the framing: the Nile is the established answer at about 6,650 km, while the Amazon, the largest river by water volume, measures roughly 6,400 km, or up to 6,992 km if a disputed 2007 survey is accepted.

  • By convention and most references, the Nile leads on length.
  • The Amazon is unambiguously the largest river by discharge.
  • The dispute is about where the river starts, not the underlying data.

A careful answer names both rivers and the measurement dispute instead of hiding it inside one confident number.
