1. What consens.io is

consens.io sends your query to several third-party AI language models in parallel, shows their answers side by side, and can generate a consolidated consensus answer. The service is operated by the provider named in the Imprint (the "operator"). It is currently a free, non-commercial project provided as is, without any guaranteed availability, response quality, or support.

2. Processing by third-party AI providers

This is the most important thing to understand about consens.io: when you send a query, your prompt and any attachments are transmitted to the AI providers behind the models you select. Depending on your selection, these are OpenAI (USA), Anthropic (USA), Google (USA), Mistral AI (France), xAI (USA), and DeepSeek (People's Republic of China). Each provider processes your content under its own terms and privacy policy, and this may include processing outside the EU/EEA, including in countries without an EU adequacy decision, such as China when you use DeepSeek models.

By using consens.io and selecting models, you instruct us to transmit your content to the selected providers and you accept this processing. If you do not want your content to reach a particular provider, deselect its models before sending a query. Do not enter personal data, secrets, or confidential information in prompts.

7. Public sharing of consensus answers

Logged-in users can publish a consensus answer as a public, read-only page ("shared page"). Creating a shared page is always an explicit, opt-in action; nothing is published automatically.

It does not contain your name, e-mail address, or any other account data, and it is not publicly linked to your account.
  • By creating a shared page you confirm that the question and any content you contributed to it do not infringe third-party rights (including copyright and privacy rights) and are not unlawful.

Reports are stored only as an anonymous counter per reason (for example "inaccurate", "harmful", "spam"); we do not store the reporter's IP address, browser data, or any other personal data with a report. Repeated reports automatically remove a page from search-engine indexing until we have reviewed it. There is no obligation to act on every report, but we review reported pages with priority.
